The Story

From federal prison
to Costa Rica

I grew up in Calgary, labelled "difficult" from the start. ADHD, school suspensions, shoplifting as a kid. Before I was a teenager I was already stealing cars, doing robberies. By fourteen, juvenile detention.

At twenty-four I moved to Arizona to open my first restaurant. A real dream. By twenty-six, addiction had swallowed it whole and I was back in Calgary — this time sliding into the drug trade. I stayed there until thirty-three, when I was arrested and sent to federal prison.

I got out and built something legitimate — a real tire shop, a proper business, a second chance. Then I gave that away too. Addiction took it just like it took everything else.

"I looked at my life and thought — if this is all there is, what's the point?"

About two years later came the overdose. I was found in a basement, unresponsive, after twelve hours. What happened next, I've never been able to fully explain.

In the coma, I was in a black hole — total darkness, no sound, no light. Then a brightness began to open above me. And a hand came down into that darkness. I reached up and grabbed it. And I woke up.

I spent months in physio and cognitive therapy rebuilding the most basic motor functions and cognitive abilities — things most people never have to think about. And when I was steady enough to stand, my mother pointed me toward Choose Again, a spiritual recovery center in Costa Rica. I arrived broken. I left transformed.

Today I live in Costa Rica as a facilitator at Choose Again. I work with people one-on-one. I facilitate circles. I cook for the center — full circle. And every single morning, I practice what I teach.

"I am grateful for all of it. Every arrest, every loss, every relapse — it all led me here. To a life where I get to choose love over fear in every moment, by watching my mind and practicing the six steps."
